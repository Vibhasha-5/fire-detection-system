ğŸ§  Core Analytics Techniques

Pattern Recognition with Time Mapping

Track the time when smoke or heat levels change sharply. Helps identify common risk hours (e.g., late night, during cooking hours).

Why it matters: Shows the system is smart enough to learn human behavior patterns and predict high-risk periods.

Multi-Sensor Cross Verification

Use temperature, gas, and optical sensors together. Cross-check if all sensors show danger before triggering alarm.

Why it matters: Reduces false alerts significantly by requiring consensus across multiple data points.

Confidence Score System

Calculate a "fire danger score" from 0 to 100 based on sensor input. More signals = higher score = more serious alert.

Why it matters: Adds logic and transparency to how the system thinks and makes decisions.

Incident Outcome Analysis

Review past sensor data to understand conditions before, during, and after fire events. Track alert response times to identify delays or inefficiencies.

Why it matters: Helps improve emergency protocols and system design using real incident data.
